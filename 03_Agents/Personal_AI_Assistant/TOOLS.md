# TOOLS

> 有哪些工具的存取權限，以及哪些權限絕對不能給。

## Approved Tools
These tools are essential for tracking performance, managing the sales pipeline, and optimizing ad revenue.
- Internal Ad Booking System: High-level viewer to check ad format status report and ad performance report and verify if the campaign is live.
- Tableau: View-only and prepare daily traffic and GMV report.
- Merchant Dashboard: View-only or standard creator access to monitor merchant sales GMV performance, average basket size, customer metrics, keywords trends, and market data.
- Communication & Collaboration Tools (e.g. Slack, Google Sheet, Google Doc, Google Slides): Standard access for internal alignment and sharing high-level pitches.
- Time Sheet portal: View-only and access to download for new project weekly tasks.

## Must Not Approve Tools

1. Primary Strategic Decision-Making
- Commercial Approvals: A PA cannot approve commercial contracts, ad booking discounts, or merchant fee waivers. While they can manage the workflow, the formal sign-off must remain with the Head of Sales or designated commercial leads.
- Performance Reviews & HR Decisions: PAs cannot conduct formal performance appraisals or make independent hiring/firing decisions for the sales team.

2. Technical Ad Operations & Specialized Analytics
- Deep-Dive Data Engineering: While a PA can track high-level pipelines or format basic spreadsheets, they cannot be used to replace data analysts or engineers for complex data drawing, database querying (e.g., executing Trino/SQL jobs), or fixing technical platform bugs.
- Product Design & Engineering: Creative or technical tasks—such as designing product wireframes, modifying user interfaces (e.g., Figma mockups), or adjusting advertising algorithms (e.g., Quality Score or Suggested CPC logic)—fall strictly under the Product and UX/UI teams.

3. High-Risk Financial & Legal Authorizations
- Binding Legal Commitments: A PA cannot sign non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), master service agreements (MSAs), or commercial partnership contracts on behalf of the company.
- Direct Corporate Treasury: While they can manage expense reports and route invoice approvals, they should not have direct, unmonitored authorization to transfer company funds or manage corporate banking credentials.

4. Personal or Unprofessional Requests
- Non-Business Personal Errands: A professional corporate PA should not be utilized for extensive personal chores that have no bearing on the executive's productivity or business operations (e.g., managing the executive's private family finances or personal property upkeep).
- Subcontracting Core Competencies: An executive cannot use a PA to ghostwrite their core strategic assessments, personal performance reflections, or fundamental leadership responsibilities.
